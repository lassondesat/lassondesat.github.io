OUR SUBTEAMS

LASSAT is proud to have members from a wide range of programs and disciplines, ranging from Space Engineering
and Computer Science, to Digital Media and Statistics. Regardless of skill level, we are always excited to welcome new members.
Our hope is for every member of LASSAT to leave with new knowledge and skills that they can apply to their future endeavours.
Some of our subteams and their work packages are as follows.

Payload

Configuring and implementing the payload for use with our OBC and transmittor.

Attitude Control Systems

Designing and implementing magnetorquers to be used to fix satellite orientation in orbit.

Mechanical

Designing the satellite structure within 3U specifications.

Onboard Power Design

Designing power PCB and solar panels to supply instruments with power.

Communications

Designing and building an antenna system with the ability to send and recieve information and photos.

Software Development

Programming OBC using a Real Time Operating System to bring together onboard components

Who we are

About

LASSAT

We are a satellite design team from the Lassonde School of Engineering at York University in Toronto. We are currently building the LasSat, a CubeSat who's mission is to carry out an outreach mission, in hopes of it being used as a global educational tool. We are competing in the Canadian Satellite Design Challenge, a national university-level satellite building competition.

Throughout this competition we are also expanding our Outreach Program, an education program geared towards students from K-12, designed to help them learn about STEM in a more fun and engaging way, inspiring the next generation of engineers.

Mission Statement

The Lassonde School of Engineering established in 2013 and became the latest engineering school in Canada. Our school offers the only undergraduate Space Engineering program across Canada. Being the only specialized undergraduate program in Canada, our students have a huge passion on creating an impact in the space industry. We want to apply what we have learned in our program and prove to others what we can achieve. Our team consists of a group of passionate students from different streams of engineering who are willing to learn.

This is our second year participating is the CSDC competition. The main scientific objective of our mission this year is to monitor greenhouse gases building up within the atmosphere using an Argus Infrared Spectrometer provided by Thoth Technology Inc. As global warming becomes a more pressing issue we aim to better the understanding of its causes and hopefully aid in a solution to ensure a future for our planet. Our secondary payload is FIPEX (ϕ-(Phi=Flux)-Probe-Experiment), a scientific instrument that will be used to measure time resolved behavior of atomic oxygen in the lower thermosphere designed by Technische Universität Dresden from Germany. Atomic oxygen causes surface erosion on LEO satellites and spacecraft glow on crafts re-entering into the atmosphere, thus it’s important to better understand and model the atomic oxygen present in our atmosphere.

Furthermore, we managed to make our own magnetorquers using our in-house made magnetorquer winder. Making our own magnetorquer is a unique and cheaper alternative not undertaken by other participants, allowing us to stretch our limited budget. These magnetorquers will be our technological payload.

Payload Implementation

Configuring and Implementing the payload for use with our OBC and transmittor

Attitude Control Systems

Designing and implementing magnetorquers to be used to fix satellite orientation in orbit

Mechanical Design

Designing the satellite structure within 3U spec

Onboard Power Design

Designing power PCB and solar panels to supply instruments with power

Communications

Designing and Building antenna system with methods to send and recieve packets

Software Development

Programming OBC using a Real Time Operating System to bring together onboard components

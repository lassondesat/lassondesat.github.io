Joining LASSAT

Lassat

Lassat is York University and the Lassonde School of Engineering's official Student Satellite Design Team. We compete in the annual Canadian Satellite Design Challenge, and rely on students from all programs when designing and implementing the steps necessary to compete.

Unlike many university competitions which are focused on only one department, successful teams in the CSDC are comprised of students from many disciplines â€“ not just from Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, and Computer), but Science (Computer Science, and the department of any science instruments which are flown), and usually benefit by having students from Management, Commerce, or Education departments.

This is a quote from the CSDC website, and highlights an important goal for our team. We want to build a team with a range of abilites, in order to have the most capable satellite design team possible.

We are about to enter a new design cycle, which means it's the prefect time for interested students to join and learn about CSDC and how the team and competition work. New to CSDC this year is the addition of specified requirements for the payload design, and we are still waiting on the official mission requirements.
New to LASSAT this year, we are introducing a new subset of the team, for those interested in space mission but without the skills or interest in satellite design or building. Lead by our new Communications Officer, we are proud to introduce the new LASSAT Media, Outreach, and Education branch. Unknown to most, CSDC also has an outreach component that would help us secure a top spot in the competition, and we are excited to grow this portion of our team and give non science and engineering students an opportunity to participate input CSDC as well.

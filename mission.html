About Us

Founded in 2014 as CSDC@YorkU, LASSAT was created to compete in the Canada-wide Canadian Satellite Design Challenge (CSDC), but has since grown into a hub for all things related to space technology. We consist of two core subteams, our satellite design team and our outreach team. These subteams are working together to not only compete in CSDC, but to create a catalog of workshops and talks for students from grades K-12, and offer them a unique opportunity to learn about space technology from our students.

CSDC

The Canadian Satellite Design Challenge (CSDC) is an annual competition for university students, both graduate and undergraduate, to compete and build a 'cubesat' - a small, fully operational satellite - built to conduct a science mission.

CSDC began in January 2011, and was modelled after other, existing university competitions, but has additional objectives and benefits icorporated, to add to the educational experience. The challenge gives participating students an opportuinty to work on a mission in a simulated environment, allowing them to experience proposing, managing, and working on a real space mission.

The CSDC Management Society acts like a space mission Customer, akin to a national space agency (like the CSA or NASA). Each team acts like a Prime Contractor, and is responsible for managing and implementing their mission. With project structure such as a Critical Design Review and required deliverable documents, the CSDC provides a foundation in project management which is rarely encountered in the university curriculum.

This is the 5th year of CSDC (CSDC-5!) and Lassonde is excited to be participating again. This year's mission will create new and exciting challenges for LASSAT, and we are excited to compete. More information on this year's mission will become available over the coming weeks/months.

Outreach Program

This year, LASSAT has it's first ever dedicated Media & Outreach Team. As mentioned above, our aim is to create a catalog of workshops and talks designed to inspred students from K-12 to pursue a path in STEM. We are excited to already have partnerships with several schools, and are excited to work with teachers and staff to create the best possible opportunities for kids to gain exposure to new fields.

Our Outreach Program is structured to work within and enrich a classroom's already existing curriculum. We plan to do this by creating hands on workshops for students to take part in a dn making the classroom a more engaging place.

If you're located in the Greater Toronto Area and your school is interested in partnering with LASSAT, please contact us at pr@lassat.ca

Mission for CSDC-5

This year's challenge is to build a "Selfie Sat" - a CubeSat that can capture photos of the earth with an area between 40x40km to 100x100km, that can be accessed by amateur radio operators (AROs) on earth. The photo would be down linked to the operator immediately. Our goal is to create an online interface that allows users track and interact with the satellite.

We hope that our completed satellite can serve as an educational tool on a global scale, allowing classrooms worldwide to interact with and learn about.
